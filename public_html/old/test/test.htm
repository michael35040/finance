What is Allocated Precious Metal?

Allocated precious metals like gold and silver are legally owned by an individual investor, not a bank.  The actual metals are stored in a secure vault facility and allocated to a private party.  As a result, allocated bullion is safe from insolvency, it is physical metal that is your legal property.

When you purchase gold and silver on the Elemetal Vault bullion exchange, you own the metal, it is yours to hold or sell, even as its in transit to the vault. Your metal belongs to you until you choose to sell it or settle your account using one of our versatile settlement options.

A point of clarity while your gold and silver are allocated, they are not segregated. Frankly, we just dont see the benefit to investors. Segregated storage takes time and, more importantly, costly labor that would increase your fees. An ounce of gold is an ounce of gold, and paying to let it sit on a separate shelf doesnt improve its security or value. Allocating your investments without segregating them is one of the many ways we help you save on premiums. Its the kind of advantage you get when you work with the Elemetal family of companies a group of businesses that has been leading the precious metal industry for years.

Allocated storage is the preferred vaulting method of investors because of its many advantages, including:

          Safety  Keeping a portion of your precious metal investments on-hand makes sense, yet there is merit in storing your larger holdings elsewhere. When your gold and silver is stored in a secure vault, its guarded by the best technology and infrastructure available.

          Ease of Liquidation  If you decide to take delivery of your allocated bullion, or prefer to withdraw funds, we make it very simple! Well wire funds to the account on file, ship OPM bullion to you discreetly and securely, or you can shop at Provident Metals.

          Insurance  When you choose to trade and store precious metals with Elemetal Vault, a global leader in secure vault storage, your bullion is kept insured. The cost to insure your bullion is included in the storage charge that you pay when you trade with us.

          Long-Term Benefits  Many precious metals investors think in broader terms about the future, economic risks, and other unforeseen events. Allocated gold that is stored securely can always be obtained or liquidated quickly and easily should the need arise.

Quality metal, and service you can trust

The companies in the Elemetal, LLC family hold the highest possible industry certifications.

When you buy and sell gold and silver online with Elemetal Vault, you are trading high quality metal on the most secure platform available. Thanks to our extensive network,  Elemetal, LLC and its subsidiaries hold quality and standards certification from the following organizations:

LBMA Good Delivery Certification means that the precious metals you trade on Elemetal Vault meet all industry standards for quality and purity. To meet LBMA Good Delivery standards, bars must meet specific requirements for fineness and metal content. Our gold and silver bars are marked to indicate serial number, refiners hallmark, fineness and the year in which the metal was manufactured.

In addition to appearing on the LBMA Good Delivery list, Elemetal subsidiary Ohio Precious Metals (OPM) is a COMEX Good Delivery certified. As part of the New York Mercantile Exchange, its commodity exchange division, and the Commodity Exchange, Inc. (COMEX), qualifying products are subject to designated market rules and regulations which focus primarily on metal purity and weight.

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) was created in 1947 to facilitate the international coordination and unification of industrial standards. ISO 9001 certification indicates that a company has met all standards for quality management, and maintains those standards by completing a yearly audit to ensure that products and services are safe, reliable and of good quality.

Additionally, inclusion on the LBMA Good Delivery list requires that a company produce gold or silver for a minimum of three years, meet minimum requirement amounts for annual production, have a net worth of at least $24 million and meet several other standards for quality and security.
